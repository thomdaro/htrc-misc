Administration of Jimmy Carter, 1978
The President transmitted to the Con-
gress the report on international transfers
of technology required by section 24 (c)
of Public Law 95-92, the International
Security Assistance Act of 1977.
The President departed Plains, Ga.,
and traveled to Boise, Idaho, where he
stayed overnight.
August 22
The President began a 3-day raft trip
down the Middle Fork of the Salmon
River in Idaho.
August 24
The President departed Idaho and
traveled to the Grand Teton National
Park in Wyoming for a week's stay.
